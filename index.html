FAQ
What is this? How do I use it?

It's a quick way to share events. Just open a new page, fill out the form, copy a link, and send it to friends! They can save the event to their calendars, or take a screenshot :))

Can I trust these event details?

It's as trustworthy as the person/page who sent it to you. Anyone can easily make a fake event, or edit the details of an existing event before forwarding it. Make sure you trust the person who sent you this event

Do you store my data?

No. The event details are stored in the link; you don't need an account or anything else. This site doesn't even have a database; there's no data to store! Nor are there cookies, tracking, ads, or anything else.

Does GitHub store my data?

Yes. GitHub keeps a log of IP addresses who access the site, and might store other server-side data such the URL you visited. Please consult GitHub's policies for details.

Why is my link in Chinese?

Some platforms support sending links, but have a character limit (e.g. Instagram has a character limit when sharing links to your story). It turns out that by compressing the URL and using a wider range of Unicode characters, we can fit a longer event into a short link. Unicode has a lot of Chinese/Asian characters, so the links look Chinese! It's almost guaranteed to be nonsense to a Chinese reader though.

Can I use this site to share community/not-for-profit events?

Yes! Please go ahead

What about commercial/for-profit events?

Yes! Go ahead

What about sharing private events with friends & family?

Yes! Go ahead, just be mindful that anyone could forward the link to new people

No. By analogy, consider an event organised by mailing physical invitations. Invitations are written on paper, distributed, potentially showcased in public, and then read by recipients, who might use some reading glasses. This website performs the function of the paper and reading glasses, i.e. it is the medium on which the event is written, and the means by which it is read.
